Physics & Math

1. Coordinate System & Geometry

Camera views balloon from distance d_cam at look-down angle θ. Constraint: bgDist = camDist + ballAlt / sin(θ).

2. Surface Normal & Reflection

Normal: N = -d_view·w + cam_right·u + cam_up·v. Reflected ray R = I - 2(I·N)N. R_z > 0 = sky, R_z < 0 = ground.

3. Key Insights

  • Center = retroreflector: Center reflects sky, not ground.
  • Hottest point NOT at bottom: Shortest slant path is below center but above limb.
  • Atmospheric shortcut: Balloon’s reflected path can be shorter than direct camera→ground.

4. Sensor Simulation

The sensor renders at native resolution with the specified HFOV. Digital zoom crops the center 1/Z fraction and scales with nearest-neighbor interpolation, preserving pixelation. Sub-pixel objects (radius < 0.5 px) use area-weighted fill factor f = πr² to blend balloon and background temperatures: T_pixel = f·T_ball + (1-f)·T_bg. Fixed-seed PRNG noise is added to the temperature map before colormap application.

5. Ground Exposure Mode

Sets display range tightly around the apparent ground temperature T_bg ± 0.55·ΔT_ground. Ground pixels span the full black-to-white range. Temperatures outside this range (sky reflections, cold sky) saturate to the display limits. In black-hot mode, cold reflected-sky pixels clip to white, accurately simulating how real FLIR AGC (Automatic Gain Control) exposes for the dominant scene element.

6. Altitude-Corrected Atmospheric Extinction

Atmospheric MWIR extinction is dominated by water vapor and aerosol scattering, both decreasing exponentially with altitude: β(z) = β0 · exp(-zASL / H), where β0 is the sea-level reference extinction coefficient and H is the scale height (≈2 km, water-vapor dominated). At the ground (ASL = Ground ASL), the actual extinction is β0 · exp(-groundASL / H)—lower when the ground is higher. For a slant path from altitude zA to zB (ASL) with path length L, the optical depth has a closed-form solution:

optical_depth = β0 · L · H / |Δz| · |exp(-zA/H) - exp(-zB/H)|

For near-horizontal paths (|Δz| < 1 m): optical_depth = βlocal · L where βlocal = β0 · exp(-zASL/H).

Why H is a separate parameter: β0 sets the magnitude of extinction; H sets its vertical structure. Two atmospheres with the same β0 but different H behave very differently: at H = 1.5 km (dry desert), extinction drops fast and air at 15,000 ft is nearly transparent; at H = 3 km (humid/hazy), significant extinction persists to high altitude. H depends on the vertical distribution of water vapor and aerosols—driven by weather, humidity, and geography—so it cannot be derived from β0 alone.

  • Raising Ground ASL shifts all paths to higher absolute altitudes with thinner air, reducing extinction.
  • At H = 10+ km the model converges to constant-β (flat atmosphere approximation).
  • The camera→balloon path at altitude is nearly transparent; most extinction happens near the ground.

7. Path-Dependent Atmospheric Emission

In the radiative transfer equation L = Lsource·τ + Latm·(1−τ), the atmospheric emission term Latm depends on the air temperature along the path. Dense warm air near the ground emits more than thin cold air at altitude, so different paths have different effective emission temperatures.

Air temperature follows the lapse rate: T(z) = Tground − Γ·(z − zground), where Γ ≈ 6.5 K/km (ISA standard). The effective emission temperature for a path is the air temperature at an “emission-weighted mean altitude”—biased toward the lower, denser end:

zeff = zlow + H·[1 − x·e−x / (1−e−x)], where x = Δz / H

The same scale height H from the extinction model (section 6) appears here because air density controls both absorption and emission: denser air absorbs more and emits more. H sets how fast density falls with altitude—at small H (dry desert), the emission weighting is strongly biased toward the low end of the path; at large H (humid), weighting spreads more evenly.

  • Camera→balloon (high altitude): Teff ≈ 255 K — cold, thin air dominates.
  • Balloon→ground: Teff ≈ 275 K — weighted toward warmer ground-level air.
  • Horizontal at ground: Teff = Tground exactly.
  • This closed-form approximation has <1 K error vs. the full path integral.

8. Rerecord (Overexposure + Dual-Gaussian PSF)

Simulates re-recording a FLIR display with a camera. Physically motivated pipeline:

  1. Monitor gamma → physical light: Display pixel values are converted to linear physical light via monitor gamma: L = (pixel/255)2.2.
  2. Overexposure (always applied): Monitor white is physically brighter than camera saturation—this is basic photometry, not a bloom effect. Pixels below the knee (0.7 linear, ≈ pixel 212) are untouched; pixels above are ramped by 2(4 + EV). At EV=0, white = 5.5× camera saturation. Without a wide PSF, this excess simply clips away at saturation.
  3. Focus blur: Gaussian blur in linear light space simulates camera defocus.
  4. Dual-Gaussian PSF: A real lens PSF has a sharp core + wide tails: PSF = (1−f)·G(σcore) + f·G(σbloom). The Bloom% slider controls f (veiling glare fraction). At f=0, all light stays in the sharp core and overexposed pixels just clip to white—no visible bloom. As f increases, more energy spreads widely: the 5.5× excess from white pixels pushes neighbors above saturation, growing the white region. The transition from 0% to 1% is smooth because only 1% of energy shifts from core to tails.
  5. Camera noise: Small random noise in linear light space models the recording camera’s sensor noise.
  6. Clipping + inverse gamma: Values are clamped at 1.0, then converted back via pixel = clipped1/2.2 × 255.

9. Sensor Noise Model

Models a cooled MWIR sensor (MTS-B style) with two components: dominant per-pixel random noise (uniform fine grain) plus very faint row banding (8% weight, subtle horizontal lines from readout timing). This matches the noise profile of real MQ-9 Reaper FLIR footage, which uses a cooled InSb detector with good NUC (Non-Uniformity Correction) — resulting in mostly uniform noise without the column striping or spatial gradients seen in uncooled sensors. All noise uses a fixed-seed PRNG for deterministic output. Magnitude is absolute (Kelvin), not display-range-relative.
